# Paixão — Creator & Influencer Outreach
**Goal:** Find adult-lifestyle creators who authentically align with Paixão's values, convert them into founding members or brand partners, and let them tell their audience about Paixão in their own voice.

**Philosophy:** Do NOT pay for generic shoutouts. Find people who would genuinely want this, give them founding access, and let them speak honestly. Authentic creator content outperforms paid placement 5:1 in trust-sensitive niches.

---

## Ideal Creator Profile

### Core Criteria (must-have)
| Criteria | Detail |
|----------|--------|
| **Age** | 25–45 (reflects target audience) |
| **Audience demographic** | Canadian-heavy OR Alberta-specific; adults 24–50 |
| **Follower range** | 3,000–80,000 (micro and mid-tier) — avoid mega influencers in this niche; authenticity > reach |
| **Content category** | Adult lifestyle, sex-positive education, luxury lifestyle, relationship/dating content, kink/BDSM community, couples content |
| **Platforms** | X/Twitter, OnlyFans (for reaching their subscriber base), Reddit, FetLife, Instagram (if their account is SFW-enough) |
| **Tone** | Thoughtful, consent-forward, values-led — NOT shock content |
| **Community engagement** | Replies to comments, genuine audience relationship, not just broadcast |

### Strong-match archetypes
1. **Sex educator** — Creator who teaches about consent, relationships, healthy sexuality. Their audience trusts their recommendations. Perfect alignment with Paixão's consent-first design.
2. **Lifestyle couple** — Couple who documents their open/poly/lifestyle journey authentically. They attract exactly Paixão's audience.
3. **Alberta/Western Canada creator** — Any creator with a specifically Canadian or Alberta-rooted audience, even if tangentially lifestyle-adjacent (e.g., a luxury lifestyle blogger in Edmonton). Geographic relevance is underrated.
4. **Kink community voice** — FetLife / Twitter presence with a respected voice in the BDSM or kink community. These communities have tight trust networks; one respected endorsement travels far.
5. **Dating/relationship coach** — Someone who advises on modern relationships (especially non-monogamy or adult dating). They're already educating the exact audience Paixão serves.

### Disqualifying factors
- Content that's exploitative, non-consensual in theme, or shock/degradation-focused (misaligns with brand)
- Audience that skews under 25 (age-appropriate platform requirements + brand fit)
- Creator with a history of spam endorsements or pay-per-post-only mentality
- Accounts with bot-inflated follower counts (check engagement rate; healthy = 3–8% for mid-tier)

## Where to Find Them

### X / Twitter
- Search: `#SexPositive Alberta`, `#ConsentMatters Canada`, `#AdultCreator Canada`, `#LifestyleCouple`
- Advanced search: filter by location (Canada), high engagement in last 30 days
- Look at who FetLife community leaders follow on Twitter
- Search OnlyFans top Canadian creators then find their Twitter accounts (most cross-post)

### FetLife
- Browse local groups for Alberta/Edmonton/Calgary — look for group members who post thoughtfully and have 100+ friends
- Search "educator," "event organizer," "community builder" in profiles
- People who run or promote their own lifestyle events are golden — they already have the audience

### Reddit
- Mods and top contributors in r/alberta_lifestyle, r/swinging, r/polyamory, r/sex_positive_community — these people have community credibility
- Users with high karma in relevant subs who write thoughtful, values-led posts

### Instagram
- Search: "sex positive," "consent education," "lifestyle couple," "Alberta" (as location tag)
- Filter: accounts with 5K–50K followers, recent posts, decent comment engagement
- Note: Instagram creators will need to keep any Paixão content SFW — they can tease without explicit details

### OnlyFans + Fansly
- These platforms have public search/discover pages
- Look for Canadian creators in the "lifestyle" or "couple" categories
- Their audience overlap with Paixão's target is extremely high
- They're accustomed to brand partnerships and will understand the compensation model

## Compensation Model (Suggested)

| Tier | What you offer | What you ask |
|------|---------------|--------------|
| **Founding gift** | Free founding membership (CA$39 value) | 1 genuine post/story about why they joined |
| **Partner tier** | Free founding + 20% affiliate commission on referrals | 2–3 posts over the campaign + story mention |
| **Ambassador tier** | Free founding + revenue share on event tickets sold via their code | Ongoing content partnership; dedicated event invites |

**Affiliate tracking:** Use UTM parameters + unique promo codes (e.g., `CREATORNAME` at checkout gives them tracking). Tools: Rewardful, Tapfiliate, or manual Stripe tracking for this early stage.

## Outreach Cadence

- **Days 1–2:** Research and shortlist 15–20 creators
- **Days 2–3:** Send first 10 DMs
- **Day 5:** Follow up on non-responses (one follow-up only — never spam)
- **Days 7–9:** Send second batch of 10 DMs
- **Day 11:** Any creators who replied should have received founding access and a clear brief on what you're asking for
- **Days 12–14:** Creator content ideally live before campaign end; reshare anything they post

## Final Notes

**The single biggest mistake in creator outreach:** Treating creators like billboard space. The adult lifestyle space is a trust economy. Creators who endorse something they don't believe in lose their audience's trust fast. Only partner with people who would genuinely use this.

**The biggest opportunity:** A single respected voice in the Alberta kink/lifestyle community saying "I joined Paixão, here's why" will outperform CA$500 in Meta ads to that same audience. Invest the time.
